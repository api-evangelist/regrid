UC Berkeley researchers used Regrid's standardized parcel data to map groundwater inequality across California's Central Valley, linking over 12,000 wells to more than 10,000 cropland parcels. They found the top 10% of well owners control 46% of total groundwater capacity, providing a baseline for tracking equity under California's Sustainable Groundwater Management Act.
